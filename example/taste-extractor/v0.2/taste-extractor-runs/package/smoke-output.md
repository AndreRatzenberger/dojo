## Transfer contract

- Preserve: silence-led density (P1), one compact gesture (P2), subdued body plus single accent (P3), and family resemblance with bounded variation (P4).
- Adapt freely: synthesis method, pitch region, source material, duration, and loudness—none is established by the evidence.
- Productive tensions: noticeable, not intrusive (P2–P3); distinct, not unrelated (P4); sparse, not empty of information (P1–P3).
- Do not transfer: the words, exact colors, shapes, layouts, or any literal sonic spelling of dot/square/dash.
- Avoid: melodies, multi-hit ornament, glossy reverb, cinematic swells, escalating “success/warning/error” semantics not supplied by the source, or three unrelated timbres.
- Accessibility, correctness, and operational clarity outrank extracted style and fidelity. Any accompanying error, identity, consent, fee, security, permission, or risk language must remain literal.

## Production handoff

Create three neutral variants pending final state names:

| Variant | Shared body | Sole differentiator |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Short, dry, subdued event (P1–P3) | Softest and most compact accent |
| 2 | Same core timbral family and contour (P4) | Firmest, most clipped accent |
| 3 | Same core timbral family and contour (P4) | Slightly more sustained, smooth accent |

Do not imply an urgency hierarchy until state meanings are supplied.

Production sequence:

1. Build one restrained, damped core event (P1–P3).
2. Derive all variants from that core (P4).
3. Change only the accent envelope/articulation (P3–P4).
4. Test recognition, blind differentiation, audibility, and non-fatiguing playback on target devices.
5. Map variants to states only after their operational meanings are known.
