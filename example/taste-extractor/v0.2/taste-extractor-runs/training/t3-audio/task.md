# T3 — Audio piece to incident-retrospective facilitation

Listening notes for a six-minute instrumental piece:

- 00:00–00:40: one dry wooden pulse every five seconds; the room tone remains audible.
- 00:40–01:50: a low bowed note answers each pulse two beats late, never at the same pitch twice.
- 01:50–02:05: the first exact unison, held briefly, followed by six seconds of silence.
- 02:05–03:35: three independent fragments overlap around the original slow pulse; density rises while the shared anchor stays legible.
- 03:35–04:10: one fragment enters early and collides. The players do not restart; the collision becomes a descending phrase that gradually restores spacing.
- 04:10–05:20: the opening call-and-response returns with roles exchanged and shorter gaps.
- 05:20–06:00: elements drop out one at a time. The last pulse is followed by room tone, not a final chord.

The downstream artifact is a repeatable 30-minute incident-retrospective meeting for five people. Another agent will facilitate it.

Extract the audio piece's taste and translate it into an implementation-ready facilitation handoff without musical theatre, sound effects, or decorative music language.
