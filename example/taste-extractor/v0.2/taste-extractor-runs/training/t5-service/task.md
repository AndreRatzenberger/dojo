# T5 — Service ritual to asynchronous onboarding flow

Observed check-in ritual at a twelve-room guesthouse:

- The host greets the guest by name, then leaves ten quiet seconds to set down bags before asking questions.
- Only three facts are requested initially: room name, breakfast time, and whether help carrying anything is needed.
- The key is handed over with one spoken orientation sentence. Detailed house information remains on a small card in the room.
- If a room is not ready, the host says so directly, offers tea or luggage storage, gives a specific next update time, and personally closes the loop.
- Returning guests skip the orientation unless something changed.
- The ritual ends when the guest chooses a direction; the host does not add a sales pitch or request a review.

The downstream artifact is an asynchronous onboarding flow for a collaborative writing app. Another agent will design it.

Extract the ritual's taste into an implementation-ready flow handoff, including first-time, returning, delayed-setup, and recovery states. Do not use hotel, key, tea, room, or hospitality decoration.
