# T2 — Prose family to password-recovery microcopy

Extracts from the same fictional essay collection:

- P1: “At six, the greenhouse clicked awake before Mara did. The vents lifted one reluctant inch. A pipe coughed in the wall.”

- P3: “He returned the borrowed drill on Tuesday. The apology arrived Thursday, carrying more parts than the drill ever had.”

Across the collection, sentences alternate plain physical observation with one restrained personification. Humor arrives late and dry. Paragraphs end on a small reversal rather than an exclamation. Concrete nouns do most of the work; emotional labels are rare.

The downstream artifact is password-recovery microcopy for a calm financial app. Another agent will write it.

Extract a reusable voice handoff without copying story facts, distinctive phrases, greenhouse imagery, objects, or sentence shapes. Do not write the final microcopy.
