## Taste thesis

A restrained editorial system creates quiet scientific intrigue through low-left micro-typography, dominant darkness and negative space, a precise amber line, and barely perceptible material texture (P1–P4).

## Transfer layers

**Do not transfer:** original title or caption wording, episode facts, logos, named motifs, or exact frame geometry.

**Adapt deliberately:** white type, near-black field, amber line, low-left placement, compact scale, and paper texture.

**Preserve:** spatial restraint (P1), stable editorial anchoring (P2), a singular separated accent (P3), and nearly imperceptible localized materiality (P4).

## Taste contract

Signature principles:

1. Preserve dominant negative space (P1).
2. Establish hierarchy through isolation, not enlargement (P1).
3. Keep title and caption variants aligned to one stable footprint (P2).
4. Use one thin amber line with clear separation from the text (P3).
5. Keep texture localized, faint, and optional (P4).

Productive tensions:

- Sparse, not vacant (P1).
- Small, not unreadable (P1).
- Consistent, not mechanically crowded (P2).
- Warmly accented, not decorative (P3).
- Tactile, not distressed (P4).

Invariants:

- Compact text remains low-left within a largely empty, near-black field (P1).
- Title and archival-caption variants share position and scale (P2).
- At most one thin amber line appears, and it does not touch the text (P3).
- Texture never reduces text legibility (P4).

Degrees of freedom:

- Choose typeface, exact margins, line length, and amber hue within the observed relationships.
- Place the accent beside or above the text; both relationships appear in the evidence (P3).
- Omit texture if it cannot remain subtle or accessible (P4).
- Adapt wrapping for episode-title length while retaining the compact footprint (P1–P2).
- Define motion and sound only after additional evidence or a separate creative decision; they are not established here.
