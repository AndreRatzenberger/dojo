## Taste handoff: science podcast episode-title template

### Evidence-backed visual language

- Near-black field with extensive negative space.
- Small white typography positioned low-left.
- Short archival captions can replace the title while retaining the same position and scale.
- A thin amber rule acts as a restrained accent.
- The rule may appear beside or above the text, stopping short rather than touching it.
- Paper texture is extremely faint and visible only in lighter regions.

Overall character: quiet, sparse, archival, precise, and editorial. The composition avoids conventional centered-title spectacle.

### Recommended template

- Use a nearly black full-frame background.
- Establish one low-left text anchor shared by episode titles and optional archival captions.
- Keep the episode title small relative to the canvas; preserve most of the frame as empty space.
- Set type in white or soft off-white with clear legibility but no glow, outline, or heavy effects.
- Limit titles to short line lengths. If wrapping is necessary, keep the block compact rather than enlarging it dramatically.
- Add a thin amber line as an optional modular element:
  - entering visually from a frame edge and ending before the text, or
  - sitting above the text with a deliberate gap.
- Keep the amber muted, not bright orange or gold.
- Apply paper texture sparingly. It should emerge only in highlights and should not make the black field visibly noisy.
- Use the same typographic hierarchy and anchor for archival captions so substitutions feel native to the system.

### Suggested component structure

1. Near-black background
2. Optional restrained highlight texture
3. Episode-title or archival-caption text block
4. Optional amber rule in one of the observed placements

Exact font family, font size, margins, line length, amber value, and texture strength cannot be recovered from the descriptions alone. These should be matched against the supplied stills during implementation rather than assigned invented numeric values.

### Important limits

The stills do not establish:

- frame order or narrative progression;
- how elements enter, exit, or transition;
- whether the amber line moves, redraws, cuts, or appears discontinuously;
- title or caption duration;
- pacing, easing, or synchronization;
- sound, music, or audio-reactive behavior.

Accordingly, this handoff recommends only a static visual system. Any animation or audio treatment requires additional evidence or a new creative decision explicitly identified as such.
