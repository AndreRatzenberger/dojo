# Candidate authoring notes

## What the baseline actually lacked

The six no-skill baselines were articulate, and every qualitative gate passed.
That was the trap: they sounded useful while scoring only 10/30 on the binary
implementation criteria.

The recurring failures were concrete:

- observations and inferences blurred together;
- principles lacked source coordinates;
- cross-medium translation copied surfaces instead of functions;
- downstream prompts, freedoms, anti-patterns, and yes/no checks disappeared;
- partial evidence was handled cautiously but not explicitly labeled
  provisional;
- high-stakes product language did not protect every literal category.

## Smallest initial technique

The initial candidate kept the v0.1 Taste Trace:

```text
source evidence -> observation -> inferred principle -> confidence ->
downstream consequence
```

It then added only what the six training failures justified: explicit partial
evidence handling, prose-axis accounting, and literal high-stakes language.
That first moved every training case to 5/5. A later full regression caught two
places where the instructions were still too easy to satisfy loosely, so the
final 30/30 belongs to the a6 candidate rather than that first green run.

## What graduation taught the candidate

The post-freeze bouts found gaps that training did not:

1. Video substitute evidence needed visible composition, space, and materials,
   not just timestamped frames plus motion and sound notes.
2. A named principle could escape the Taste Trace later in the handoff.
3. A long operational handoff could introduce normative actions outside the
   requested coordinate table.
4. Audio intake needed a lawful playable source paired with timestamped notes,
   plus explicit authorized substitutes.
5. Cross-medium translation could accidentally collapse prerequisite clearance
   into downstream decision authority or leave a uniqueness invariant implicit.
6. Product and service handoffs needed to state the full priority order, not
   merely sound operationally careful.
7. Task-supplied labels, enumerations, and recurrence counts needed exact schema
   fidelity; excluding an outlier cannot turn its observed count into zero.
8. Every requested axis row needed its own coordinate, including unknowns, and
   a requested prose passage-by-axis matrix needed a quote in every supported
   cell rather than one nearby quote per row.

Each miss produced a narrow reusable rule. None produced a domain-specific
answer key.

## Rejected and invalid responses

- The first capture set was discarded when the harness overwrote several
  artifacts with its own final-response file.
- One prose edit was rejected at 3/5 before the complete prose-axis and consent
  correction passed.
- The a3 candidate passed three fresh holdouts, but its final full training
  regression still failed one interface priority criterion and two prose
  evidence criteria. Those greens were not treated as graduation.
- The a4 and a5 freezes were burned after exact-schema, axis-coordinate, and
  per-cell quotation gaps appeared. Each affected case passed before another
  freeze was allowed.
- An append-only grading requirement was rejected as hidden because the task
  had never exposed it. The skill was not trained to guess secret criteria;
  the final custodian instead had to trace every criterion to exact task text.

The result is longer than v0.1, but the growth is attached to observed failure
modes rather than speculative framework furniture.
