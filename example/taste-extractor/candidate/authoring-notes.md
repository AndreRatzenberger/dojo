# Candidate authoring notes

## Baseline signal

Three baseline runners already produced strong results. The prose runner passed
4/5 but did not trace each major principle to concrete source evidence. That is
the observed failure the candidate must correct without degrading the strong
interface, cross-medium, or missing-source behavior.

## Smallest instruction that answers the failure

The candidate makes a `Taste Trace` mandatory:

```text
source evidence -> observation -> inferred principle -> confidence ->
downstream consequence
```

Every major principle must have a row. Unsupported principles are removed or
marked as unresolved hypotheses.

## Additional structure

The output contract formalizes useful behavior already seen inconsistently in
the baselines: source-content separation, cross-medium functional translation,
invariants versus degrees of freedom, originality guardrails, degraded-source
intake, a downstream prompt, and yes/no validation checks.
