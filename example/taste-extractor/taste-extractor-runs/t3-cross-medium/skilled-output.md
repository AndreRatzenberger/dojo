## 3. Abstractions

### Taste thesis

Quiet utility held inside an honest, human system: restrained repetition creates trust, controlled irregularity keeps it alive, one meaningful exception breaks the cadence, and evidence of recovery remains visible without displacing the thing being served.

### Transfer layers

#### Source content — do not transfer

- Bowls, cups, breakfast, food, vessels, pottery silhouettes, studio scenes, or collection photography.
- Thumbprints, clay edges, brush-painted rings, an incomplete circle as a logo, crack drawings, stitches, repair seams, or a literal three-short/one-tall composition.
- The names or story of the ceramic collection, if later supplied.

#### Surface manifestations — adapt deliberately, never by reflex

- Chalky oat and cobalt are evidence of a muted field plus one contrasting accent, not a mandated web palette.
- Rough linen and matte glaze establish low sheen and tactile modesty, not permission for background texture, grain overlays, skeuomorphism, or reduced legibility.
- Off-round form and thumb marks establish controlled variation and visible authorship, not warped cards, hand-drawn borders, handwritten type, or imprecise layout.
- The tall cup establishes a single proportional surprise, not a requirement for one arbitrarily tall page module.
- Visible repair establishes continuity and truthfulness, not a kintsugi or cracked-surface motif.

#### Portable principles — preserve

1. Content remains primary; character stays subordinate to public utility.
2. A dependable shared grammar allows small, purposeful variation.
3. Accent and emphasis are scarce enough to retain force.
4. Calm comes from separation, restraint, and low spectacle.
5. A hierarchy break occurs only when the underlying service condition earns it.
6. Recovery and prior disruption remain legible as part of the service's history.
7. Humanity is expressed through honest language and evidence, not simulated craft defects.

### Signature principles

1. **Serve first.** The status information is the equivalent of the food: it must remain unmistakably central.
2. **Reliable, not sterile.** Preserve a rigorous information system while allowing content-driven differences and plain human phrasing.
3. **One mark is enough.** Treat color, motion, or other strong emphasis as a scarce resource with a specific semantic job.
4. **Related, never cloned.** Establish one family grammar; allow modules to differ when their actual information differs.
5. **Let calm amplify the exception.** Most states remain quiet so a genuine incident can break hierarchy without theatrical alarm.
6. **Show the repair.** Make progression and resolution inspectable; recovery does not erase the event that preceded it.
7. **Keep the finish matte.** Favor a reserved, low-showmanship emotional register over glossy product theater.

### Productive tensions

- Human, not handmade-themed.
- Irregular, not unreliable.
- Spacious, not under-informative.
- Quiet, not passive or vague.
- Related, not mechanically identical.
- Distinctive, not decorative.
- Exceptional when warranted, not whimsically surprising.
- Transparent about recovery, not permanently alarming.
- Incomplete as an aesthetic relationship, not incomplete as a service-state signal.

### Invariants

- A visitor can identify the current overall service condition before encountering stylistic detail.
- Status meaning never depends on color, shape, texture, proportion, or position alone.
- Strong emphasis remains scarce and semantically earned.
- The system has a visibly coherent family grammar without forcing unequal content into equal containers.
- Active disruption may break the calm cadence; routine content may not imitate urgency.
- Incident progression, resolution, and time remain inspectable.
- No literal ceramic, breakfast, brush, linen, crack, or repair motif appears.
- Accessibility and factual clarity override every taste decision.

### Degrees of freedom

- The downstream agent may choose a warm or cool neutral field; exact oat and cobalt values are not required or established.
- Typography, grid, component architecture, icon system, and implementation stack are open.
- The scarce accent may be expressed through color, weight, scale, position, motion, or language, provided its meaning is accessible and it does not become ambient decoration.
- “Related but never identical” may appear through content-shaped height, disclosure depth, editorial rhythm, or hierarchy. It need not appear as visibly irregular geometry.
- The amount of whitespace may contract responsively for small screens or information-heavy incidents; separation must serve comprehension rather than mimic the source arrangement.
- The page may use conventional status colors and patterns when needed for immediate comprehension. Semantic convention outranks palette fidelity.
- Visible repair may be carried by an update timeline, resolution note, incident archive, or another inspectable record; the exact mechanism is open.
- Motion is optional. If used, it should clarify state change and remain restrained, reduced-motion safe, and non-theatrical.

### Target-specific operating rules

- Apply the taste across normal, degraded, partial outage, major outage, maintenance, loading, stale-data, empty, error, and resolved-history states; do not evaluate it only on a calm default view.
- Use a stable status vocabulary and pair every status with explicit text. Do not rely on a colored dot or decorative mark alone.
- Make timestamps and freshness legible. “Quiet” cannot mean hiding uncertainty or stale information.
- Let severity and impact change hierarchy, not the desire to create visual variety.
- Preserve the record of a repair through clear updates and resolution language, while distinguishing resolved history from current impact.
- Prefer ordinary, accountable sentences over either institutional fog or folksy craft language.
- Keep scanning paths conventional enough for a stressed visitor. Any unusual composition must earn its place through faster comprehension.
- Treat semantic HTML, keyboard navigation, focus visibility, contrast, zoom, screen-reader labeling, reduced motion, and responsive behavior as non-negotiable production constraints.

## 5. Handoff

### Production sequence

1. Model the page's required information and states before choosing a visual treatment: overall state, service-level state, incidents, impact, timestamps, updates, maintenance, and resolved history.
2. Establish one dependable information grammar and verify it in a routine all-operational state.
3. Define the scarce emphasis system and state exactly which operational meanings are allowed to invoke it.
4. Introduce only content-driven variation inside the shared grammar; remove variation that has no informational cause.
5. Create the exceptional-state treatment for a genuine incident, ensuring that surrounding calm increases its clarity without turning it into spectacle.
6. Make recovery legible from incident opening through resolution and history.
7. Exercise every normal, exceptional, transitional, missing-data, and failure state on desktop and narrow screens.
8. Run the rejection tests and binary validation checks below; strip any choice that behaves like pottery costume.

### Ready-to-use downstream prompt

Design and implement a public service-status page using this taste contract:

The page should feel quietly useful, materially honest in attitude, and human without becoming handmade-themed. Build one dependable information grammar, then let real content create restrained variation within it. Current status, impact, scope, timestamps, and updates are always the event; visual identity must support them and recede.

Use strong emphasis sparingly and only for an operationally meaningful state or action. Let the routine experience remain calm enough that a genuine active incident can make one clear hierarchy break. Do not use surprise as permanent branding. Preserve a visible, readable path from incident detection through updates to resolution, and keep resolved incidents discoverable without making old failure look current.

Translate the source's low-sheen, spacious restraint into clear grouping, breathing room, and low-showmanship effects. Adjust density to screen size and urgency. Express humanity through candid, precise language and content-driven differences, never through warped geometry, faux craft marks, handwritten type, or intentional imprecision.

Do not use ceramics, bowls, cups, food, oat glaze, cobalt brush strokes, incomplete circles, linen texture, thumbprints, cracks, seams, gold repair, or a literal three-quiet/one-tall composition. Exact source colors, dimensions, and textures are not part of the contract. Conventional status semantics, accessibility, correctness, and rapid comprehension outrank stylistic fidelity.

Cover all-operational, degraded, partial and major outage, maintenance, loading, stale-data, empty, error, and resolved-history states. Use explicit status text rather than color alone; expose timestamps and data freshness; support keyboard and screen-reader use, visible focus, sufficient contrast, zoom, reduced motion, and narrow screens. Before delivery, apply the rejection tests and yes/no validation checks in this handoff.

### Explicit implementation freedoms

- Choose the page architecture and visual system appropriate to the service and its users.
- Choose any accessible palette; a neutral field plus one limited accent is a relationship, not a color prescription.
- Choose typography and iconography for rapid public comprehension.
- Decide whether variation appears in scale, spacing, disclosure, wording, or component depth; keep it caused by content.
- Omit any visual analogue for the incomplete brush line if it risks implying partial completion, degraded uptime, or stale data.
- Use established status-page conventions wherever they communicate better than a novel translation.

### Rejection tests

Reject the direction if any answer is yes:

1. Would a reviewer describe it as “pottery-inspired” before describing how clearly it communicates service state?
2. Does it contain a bowl, cup, clay, brush, linen, thumbprint, crack, seam, kintsugi, or incomplete-circle motif?
3. Is strong accent or emphasis repeated so often that routine and exceptional states carry similar visual force?
4. Is a hierarchy break present when nothing operationally exceptional is happening?
5. Does simulated irregularity make alignment, controls, status semantics, or scanning less dependable?
6. Are modules varied because variation looks artisanal rather than because their information differs?
7. Does whitespace separate related facts, conceal recent updates below the fold, or slow urgent scanning?
8. Is repair represented as decoration while incident progression or resolution remains hard to inspect?
9. Does a quiet visual tone soften, euphemize, or hide impact?
10. Would removing the source story leave behind unexplained decoration rather than a coherent status experience?

### Yes/no validation checks

- [ ] Can a first-time visitor identify the current overall condition quickly in both routine and active-incident states?
- [ ] Is every status expressed in text and understandable without color, texture, motion, or position?
- [ ] Do the strongest visual and verbal emphasis correspond to the most consequential current information?
- [ ] Is that emphasis scarce enough to feel exceptional?
- [ ] Do repeated modules clearly belong to one system while varying only for content, severity, or interaction needs?
- [ ] Does the all-operational state feel calm without feeling empty, vague, or promotional?
- [ ] Does an active incident break hierarchy clearly without theatrical alarm?
- [ ] Can a visitor follow an incident from first report through updates to resolution and distinguish history from current impact?
- [ ] Are timestamps, timezone or locale treatment, and freshness understandable?
- [ ] Are loading, stale-data, empty, error, maintenance, and resolved states truthful and unambiguous?
- [ ] Is all literal pottery and repair imagery absent?
- [ ] Are gloss, texture, shadow, animation, and accent subordinate to information?
- [ ] Does the page remain readable, navigable, and semantically complete with styles, color, images, and motion independently removed?
- [ ] Does the implementation meet keyboard, focus, contrast, zoom, screen-reader, reduced-motion, and responsive requirements?

### Unresolved evidence gaps and confidence notes

- High confidence: quiet utility, sparse decoration, controlled nonuniformity, family resemblance, one cadence-breaking exception, visible repair, and low-gloss restraint are directly stated.
- Medium confidence: translating the incomplete brush line into an interface behavior. Its source role is clear, but a literal incomplete form can falsely imply partial progress or degraded state; omission is preferable when semantics are at risk.
- Medium confidence: translating tactile roughness. The prose supports modesty and low sheen, but not a particular digital texture; no texture should be introduced without a functional reason and legibility testing.
- Unknown: exact palette, values, contrast relationships, dimensions, spacing ratios, type character, repair appearance, and degree of variation across the full collection.
- Additional photographs or direct inspection could refine the source receipt, but they are not required to act on the high-confidence relational principles above.
