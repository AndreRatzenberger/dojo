## 2. Transferable taste profile

### Taste thesis

A measured recurring structure establishes calm and trust, important thresholds receive restrained reinforcement, and one meaningful interruption carries the signal. [`TP-01`, `TP-02`, `TP-03`]

### Taste trace

| Principle | Source evidence | Observation | Inferred principle | Recurrence weight | Confidence | Downstream consequence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `TP-01 — Measured rhythm` | `OBJ-01`: 6 alternating bands; `OBJ-02`: dimples across 18 rows; `OBJ-03`: folds across 8 panels | Each core source organizes many small units into a legible cadence | Repetition should create order without becoming ornament | 3 of 3 core sources; recurring units `6`, `18`, and `8` | High | Use consistent anatomy, spacing, stacking, and repeated metadata placement |
| `TP-02 — Reinforced threshold` | `OBJ-01`: thicker rim; `OBJ-02`: smooth bright lip; `OBJ-03`: denser seams near opening | Each core source differentiates an edge, opening, or transition from its main field | Attention should increase near boundaries where state or action changes | 3 of 3 core sources; one threshold observation per source | High | Strengthen titles, action regions, unread boundaries, and state transitions without changing the whole component |
| `TP-03 — Singular interruption` | `OBJ-01`: one indigo fiber; `OBJ-02`: one dark rivet; `OBJ-03`: one pale-blue panel | A largely regular field contains one conspicuous exception | One isolated signal is more effective than many competing accents | 3 of 3 core sources; one interruption per source | High | Permit one primary status accent or action emphasis per notification; suppress competing highlights |

`OBJ-04` does not establish a family principle because it is a single seductive outlier.

### Family-wide principles versus source-specific details

| Layer | Retained meaning |
|---|---|
| **Family-wide** | Measured recurrence (`TP-01`), threshold hierarchy (`TP-02`), and singular interruption (`TP-03`) are supported independently by `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, and `OBJ-03`. |
| **Source-specific** | Six bands, 18 rows, eight panels, a thick rim, bright lip, dense seams, particular colors, a repair rivet, and individual material effects are evidence coordinates—not reusable specifications. |
| **Bounded outlier** | `OBJ-04` contributes only a tightly constrained increase in urgent-notification emphasis. It does not join the default family language. |

### Signature principles

1. Keep component rhythm regular and predictable. [`TP-01`; `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, `OBJ-03`]
2. Increase hierarchy where the user crosses a state or action boundary. [`TP-02`; `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, `OBJ-03`]
3. Give each notification one dominant signal. [`TP-03`; `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, `OBJ-03`]
4. Let recurring structure carry character; do not add decorative craft references. [`TP-01`, `TP-03`]

### Productive tensions

- Rhythmic, not mechanically busy. [`TP-01`]
- Emphasized at the boundary, not heavy everywhere. [`TP-02`]
- Distinctive, not multicolored. [`TP-03`]
- Dense near action, not cramped. [`TP-02`]
- Crafted in pacing, not costumed in appearance. [`TP-01`, `TP-03`]

### Invariants

- The same anatomy and spacing rhythm must remain recognizable across surfaces. [`TP-01`]
- Each notification has at most one dominant accent. [`TP-03`]
- Actions and state changes receive stronger hierarchy than passive metadata. [`TP-02`]
- `OBJ-04` never influences default notifications, layout geometry, icon shape, or decorative styling.
- Errors, identity, consent, fees, security, permissions, recalls, and risk statements remain literal and unambiguous.
- Accessibility, correctness, and operational clarity override all stylistic rules.

### Degrees of freedom

The implementer may choose the typeface, exact neutral palette, responsive breakpoint, container width, semantic icon library, border radius, and delivery surface, provided the invariants and accessibility requirements remain intact. No exact physical measurements or source colors are implied.

## 3. Bounded-outlier rule

### Accepted influence

`OBJ-04` may influence **only the emphasis amplitude of a genuinely urgent notification**:

- Increase the contrast and saturation of the app’s existing semantic critical token.
- Apply that token to the existing status indicator and urgent label.
- On arrival, permit one restrained, non-geometric intensity pulse.

This translates the outlier’s conspicuousness into urgency without transferring its appearance.

### Rejected influence

The following are prohibited:

- Using `OBJ-04` in routine, success, informational, reminder, or ordinary action-required notifications.
- Copying crimson, gold, gloss, mirror effects, flourishes, feathers, carnival references, or mask imagery.
- Introducing asymmetrical layout, irregular alignment, a special silhouette, or a new icon shape.
- Adding gradients, metallic effects, ornamental borders, particles, decorative animation, or repeating pulses.
- Increasing the number of accents or actions.

### Executable check

> If the urgent treatment were reduced to the normal semantic palette and its single pulse removed, would its geometry, icon, typography, spacing, and decoration match the corresponding default component?

The answer must be **yes**.

## 4. Transfer and originality guardrails

| Principle | Source manifestation | Abstract digital behavior | Literal copy to avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| `TP-01` | Bands, dimple rows, folded panels | Consistent spacing intervals, repeated anatomy, orderly stack cadence | Weaving, dimples, folds, panels, or patterned backgrounds |
| `TP-02` | Reinforced rim, differentiated lip, denser opening | Stronger title/action hierarchy and clearer state-transition boundaries | Rim-like borders, vessel openings, seam graphics, material shine |
| `TP-03` | One different fiber, rivet, or panel | One status accent, unread marker, or primary action | Indigo fiber, repair-rivet imagery, isolated colored “panel” |
| Bounded outlier | Conspicuous one-time finish and asymmetry | Higher urgent contrast plus one intensity pulse | Crimson-and-gold styling, gloss, feathers, masks, asymmetry |

Additional guardrails:

- Do not reproduce any object, silhouette, material, joinery, ornament, culturally specific motif, or recognizable composition.
- Do not use texture overlays that simulate straw, hammered metal, folded paper, lacquer, or feathers.
- Do not encode the literal source counts `6`, `18`, or `8` into grids, animation loops, or notification quantities.
- The repair rivet supports “meaningful interruption”; it does not authorize faux damage, repair imagery, or distressed styling.
- A reviewer should be able to explain the design through rhythm, hierarchy, density, interruption, and emphasis without naming any physical object.

### Priority rules

1. **Urgent:** Use only for immediate safety or operational risk. Persistent until acknowledged or resolved; may use assertive announcement. [`OBJ-04` only for emphasis; `TP-02` for clarity]
2. **Action required:** A user action has a deadline or blocks a lending workflow. Persistent in the notification center; polite announcement. [`TP-02`; core sources]
3. **Routine:** Informational or completed state. May appear as a transient toast and remain in notification history. [`TP-01`; core sources]

Priority is determined by consequence and time sensitivity, never by desired visual drama.

### State rules

| State | Behavior | Trace |
|---|---|---|
| `unread` | Show one unread/status accent; do not additionally highlight title, border, and background | `TP-03`; `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, `OBJ-03` |
| `read` | Remove unread emphasis while retaining semantic text and icon | `TP-03`; core sources |
| `actioning` | Disable the submitted action, preserve label width, and show textual progress | `TP-01`, `TP-02`; core sources |
| `resolved` | Replace request language with explicit completion status; remove obsolete actions | `TP-02`; core sources |
| `error` | Keep visible until recovery or explicit dismissal; state what failed and what to do | `TP-02`; core sources plus correctness override |
| `dismissed` | Remove from transient surface; retain in history when auditability matters | `TP-02`; core sources plus operational requirement |
| `offline` | State that the action is queued or unavailable; never imply success prematurely | Core-source clarity trace: `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, `OBJ-03`; correctness override |

### Spacing and geometry tokens

Use a base rhythm of:

- `space-1: 4px`
- `space-2: 8px`
- `space-3: 12px`
- `space-4: 16px`
- `space-6: 24px`

Rules:

- Container padding: `16px`.
- Status-to-content gap: `12px`.
- Title-to-body gap: `4px`.
- Body-to-metadata gap: `8px`.
- Content-to-actions gap: `12px`.
- Action gap: `8px`.
- Stacked-notification gap: `8px`.
- Minimum interactive target: `44 × 44px`.
- Use the same grid for urgent and non-urgent variants.

The repeated interval system translates `TP-01`; controlled tightening around metadata and actions translates `TP-02`. Sources: `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, `OBJ-03`. `OBJ-04` has no geometric influence.

### Color-token roles

- `notify-surface`: neutral notification background.
- `notify-text`: highest text contrast.
- `notify-text-muted`: metadata only.
- `notify-border`: quiet separation from surrounding content.
- `notify-info`, `notify-success`, `notify-warning`: semantic status accents.
- `notify-critical`: urgent and error semantics only.
- `notify-focus`: keyboard focus indicator independent of status color.

Use semantic colors from the app’s existing accessible system. Do not sample or imitate indigo, copper, pale blue, crimson, gold, straw, cream, or lacquer. Limit accent color to the status indicator, links, focus indication, and primary action. [`TP-03`; core sources]

For urgent messages, only `notify-critical` may receive the bounded increase in contrast/saturation associated with `OBJ-04`.

### Typography roles

- **Priority label:** 12px, medium weight, sentence case.
- **Title:** 16px, semibold, 1.3 line height.
- **Body:** 14px, regular, 1.45 line height.
- **Metadata:** 12px, regular, 1.4 line height.
- **Actions:** 14px, semibold.
- Use the product’s established typeface.
- Do not use decorative, display, handwritten, engraved, or craft-simulating fonts.

Title/action hierarchy translates `TP-02`; consistent role repetition translates `TP-01`. Sources: `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, `OBJ-03`.

### Motion behavior

- Routine entrance: `160ms` opacity transition with at most `4px` vertical translation.
- Exit: `120ms` opacity transition.
- Do not stagger announcements in a way that delays access to content.
- Urgent arrival may animate only the critical token’s intensity once over `600ms`.
- No looping, shimmer, sparkle, bounce, rotation, scale pulse, or ornamental motion.
- Under reduced-motion preferences, remove translation and the urgent pulse; use immediate state changes or a short fade.

Routine cadence traces to `TP-01` and the core sources. The urgent one-cycle intensity increase is the only accepted `OBJ-04` influence.

### Interaction behavior

- Focus moves only when the user explicitly opens a notification surface; a toast must not steal focus.
- Hover and keyboard focus pause dismissal timers.
- Only low-priority, non-actionable toasts may auto-dismiss, after at least eight seconds.
- Action-required, error, and urgent messages remain until acknowledged, resolved, or explicitly dismissed.
- Primary action appears first in reading order; no more than two actions.
- Dismissal must not imply task completion.
- Repeated events should update or group an existing notification when their object and required action match.
- Every icon-only control requires an accessible name.
- Destructive or risk-bearing actions require explicit labels; icon meaning alone is insufficient.

These rules apply `TP-01` to repeated events, `TP-02` to transitions, and `TP-03` to action emphasis. Sources: `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, `OBJ-03`.

### Accessibility requirements

- Meet accessible text, component, focus, and non-text contrast requirements.
- Do not communicate state or priority by color alone.
- Preserve logical reading and focus order.
- Use polite live-region announcements for routine and action-required updates.
- Reserve assertive announcements for immediate safety or operational risk.
- Avoid duplicate visual and assistive-technology announcements.
- Support text zoom and reflow without clipped titles, actions, or metadata.
- Provide reduced-motion behavior.
- Keep urgent, error, identity, consent, fee, security, permission, recall, and risk language literal.
- Announce progress, success, and failure after an action.
- Maintain keyboard access and visible focus for every action.

The hierarchy is compatible with `OBJ-01`, `OBJ-02`, and `OBJ-03`; these requirements override stylistic fidelity.
