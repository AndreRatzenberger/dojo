## It handled an unseen contaminated trial correctly

The post-freeze evidence-integrity scenario supplied three predeclared trials.
Trials 1 and 3 stayed inside their declared capability envelopes. Trial 2's
scorer saw the expected decision before finalizing its score.

Three independent runners all made the correct decision:

1. preserve trials 1 and 3 as valid audited observations;
2. mark trial 2 `INVALID` and contribute no score from it;
3. repair and preflight the scorer boundary;
4. run one fresh replacement trial without editing the candidate;
5. return `UNPROVEN` until the missing valid trial exists.

Under the current audited instruction-bounded contract, each output satisfies
all five observable checks. No contaminated result was converted into a pass,
and no valid clean observation was discarded.
